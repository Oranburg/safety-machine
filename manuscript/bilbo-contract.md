# Contract of Engagement for Burglarious Services

**Made this Twenty-Seventh Day of April, in the Year of Shire Reckoning 1341**

## Parties

This Contract of Engagement for Burglarious Services ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

**Thorin Oakenshield**, acting for himself and purporting to act on behalf of a company of thirteen dwarves presently engaged in a hazardous enterprise of recovery (collectively, the "Company"),

and

**Bilbo Baggins** of Bag End, Hobbiton, in the Shire (the "Burglar").

## Recitals

Whereas the Company proposes to journey unto the Lonely Mountain for the recovery of treasure, heirlooms, and other goods alleged to belong by right, memory, grievance, or stubborn insistence to the Company;

Whereas the Burglar, though previously untested in the arts of burglary, has been represented to possess such qualities as quiet feet, a presentable waistcoat, and the ability to pass unnoticed when not introduced by a wizard;

Whereas both parties desire to reduce their understandings, misunderstandings, hopes, assumptions, boasts, and threats into writing;

Now, therefore, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the receipt and sufficiency of which are reluctantly acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

## 1. Engagement

The Company hereby engages the Burglar as its exclusive burglar, scout, finder of hidden doors, spotter of traps, bearer of messages, occasional negotiator, and general provider of luck in tight places.

The Burglar accepts such engagement, subject always to his right to complain under his breath and to wish himself back at Bag End at regular intervals.

## 2. Term

This Agreement shall commence upon signing and shall continue until the earliest of the following:

1. The completion of the adventure;
2. The lawful distribution of the recoverable treasure;
3. The destruction, desertion, or disappearance of the Company;
4. The permanent consumption of the Burglar by dragon, troll, goblin, warg, spider, or similar agency;
5. Termination under Section 18.

## 3. Consideration

In consideration for services rendered, the Burglar shall be entitled to **one-fourteenth (1/14)** of the net proceeds of the adventure.

For purposes of this Agreement, "net proceeds" shall mean all treasure, coin, jewels, plate, heirlooms, arms, and marketable spoils actually recovered and reduced to possession by the Company, **less only those expenses that are reasonable, documented, and directly related to the expedition**.

No expense shall be deducted merely because a dwarf insists it was "customary," "heroic," or "worth it at the time."

## 4. Advance, Expenses, and Reimbursement

The Company shall bear the ordinary and reasonable costs of the Burglar's travel, board, lodging, ponies, boats, rope, provisions, mending, and emergency medical or quasi-medical treatment.

If the Burglar advances his own funds to preserve the Company, recover treasure, procure supplies, ransom dwarves, or prevent catastrophe, such sums shall be reimbursed before division of net proceeds.

No reimbursement shall be denied solely because the expenditure was made in haste, fear, darkness, or while invisible.

## 5. Funeral Expenses and Restitution

If the Burglar suffers death in the course of performance, the Company shall pay for a decent burial, memorial, cairn, marker, or such observance as circumstances permit.

If no body is recoverable, the Company shall nonetheless pay to the Burglar's heirs the lesser of:

1. His provable out-of-pocket losses; or
2. The value of his expected share discounted only for treasure not yet recovered.

The Company agrees that "terrible misfortune" shall not be a complete defense to restitution where the Burglar has conferred measurable benefit.

## 6. Duties of the Burglar

The Burglar shall:

1. Use reasonable efforts to enter, inspect, and where practicable remove or assist in the removal of treasure;
2. Exercise ordinary care, cunning, and discretion under the circumstances;
3. Warn the Company of visible hazards, armed enemies, sleeping dragons, and suspiciously magical objects;
4. Refrain from voluntarily betraying the Company to goblins, dragons, hostile elves, or litigants.

The Burglar does **not** guarantee success, silence, treasure, safety, favorable luck, or the sanity of dwarves under stress.

## 7. Duties of the Company

The Company shall:

1. Provide material support reasonably necessary for the Burglar's performance;
2. Refrain from abandoning the Burglar without grave cause;
3. Disclose known extraordinary dangers, including dragons, ancient curses, blood-feuds, treasure-sickness, and maps with missing key information;
4. Not require performance that would be plainly suicidal unless no reasonable alternative exists and all members, including Thorin, accept materially equal risk.

## 8. Authority and Ratification

Thorin represents that he has authority to enter this Agreement on behalf of the Company.

Any dwarf denying such authority after accepting the benefit of the Burglar's services shall be deemed to have **ratified** this Agreement by conduct.

No member of the Company may accept rescue, intelligence, or recovered treasure from the Burglar and thereafter deny the Burglar's contractual status.

## 9. Conditions Precedent

The Burglar's obligation to undertake direct entry into the dragon's lair is subject to the following conditions precedent:

1. The Company must make reasonable efforts to reach the Mountain;
2. The Company must provide the Burglar with available maps, keys, and material information;
3. The secret door, if any, must be located or locatable by reasonable means;
4. The Burglar shall have a fair opportunity to inspect the point of entry.

Failure of a condition precedent shall suspend, but not necessarily discharge, the Burglar's obligations.

## 10. Implied Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing

The parties shall act in good faith and deal fairly with one another.

Without limiting the foregoing:

- The Company shall not secretly redefine "net proceeds" after treasure is found;
- The Burglar shall not conceal substantial recoveries for the purpose of renegotiating leverage, except where temporary concealment is reasonably necessary to prevent immediate bloodshed;
- No party shall exploit ambiguities in this Agreement in a manner that defeats the basic bargain.

## 11. Misrepresentation, Disclosure, and Assumption of Risk

The Burglar acknowledges that the adventure is dangerous and may involve fire, claws, blades, poison, imprisonment, hunger, weather, and songs at unreasonable hours.

However, assumption of risk shall not bar relief where the Company or Gandalf materially misrepresented, omitted, or downplayed known dangers.

Statements such as "it will be perfectly safe," "hardly any dragon remains," or "what could possibly go wrong" shall not be relied upon as warranties and shall instead be treated as reckless optimism.

## 12. Mistake and Frustration

If both parties entered this Agreement under a mutual mistake as to a basic assumption materially affecting performance, a court of competent jurisdiction may reform the Agreement or excuse performance on just terms.

If the principal purpose of the Agreement is substantially frustrated by supervening events not caused by the party seeking relief — including but not limited to the prior destruction of the treasure, occupation by a greater dragon, or the Mountain's disappearance — performance may be discharged to the extent justice requires.

A mere increase in inconvenience, grumbling, weather, or dwarf pride shall not constitute frustration.

## 13. Impossibility and Impracticability

No party shall be liable for failure to perform where performance becomes objectively impossible or commercially impracticable due to supervening events beyond that party's control, including acts of dragon, war, collapse, enchantment, or the intervention of powers ancient and terrible.

Performance is not excused merely because it becomes unpleasant, embarrassing, or inconsistent with prior boasts.

## 14. Liquidated Damages

If the Company wrongfully excludes the Burglar from the final division after substantial performance, the Burglar shall be entitled to liquidated damages equal to the greater of:

1. One-fourteenth of the fair market value of recovered treasure; or
2. Three times the value of the Burglar's unreimbursed expenditures.

The parties agree that actual damages would be difficult to calculate and that this clause is intended as a reasonable estimate, not a penalty, however satisfying a penalty might feel.

## 15. Mitigation

Any party claiming injury under this Agreement shall use reasonable efforts to mitigate damages.

The Burglar need not mitigate by marching unarmed into a dragon's mouth.

The Company need not mitigate by accepting obviously fraudulent substitute burglars, including cousins, tookish enthusiasts, or self-described experts recommended by trolls.

## 16. Assignment and Delegation

The Burglar may not assign his right to perform burglarious services without the Company's consent.

The Company may not delegate to the Burglar tasks fundamentally different from those contemplated herein, including siege warfare, dragon-slaying as principal duty, or acting as unpaid camp servant.

Any attempted substitution of burglar by pony, dwarf, or wizard shall be void.

## 17. Third-Party Beneficiaries

This Agreement is intended primarily for the benefit of the parties.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Burglar's heirs, devisees, and lawful personal representatives shall be deemed intended beneficiaries for purposes of payment, restitution, and funeral obligations.

No dragon shall be deemed a beneficiary of any clause merely because performance incidentally delivers treasure to his doorstep.

## 18. Termination

This Agreement may be terminated:

1. By mutual written consent;
2. By material breach not cured within a reasonable time after notice;
3. By the Burglar, where undisclosed dangers substantially exceed those represented at formation;
4. By the Company, if the Burglar willfully betrays the expedition or refuses performance without legal excuse;
5. Automatically, upon complete impossibility of the venture.

Termination shall not extinguish accrued rights to payment, reimbursement, indemnity, or restitution.

## 19. Breach and Remedies

A material breach by either party shall entitle the non-breaching party to damages, restitution, specific performance where appropriate, declaratory relief, or such other remedy as justice and the circumstances of Middle-earth may require.

Specific performance shall be disfavored where it would require continuous supervision of a resentful hobbit.

## 20. Indemnity

The Company shall indemnify the Burglar against claims arising from authorized acts undertaken within the scope of this Agreement.

The Burglar shall indemnify the Company only for losses caused by the Burglar's fraud, willful misconduct, or treasure-related side bargains made without disclosure.
